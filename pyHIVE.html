Providing six more feature extraction algorithms for biomedical images with pyHIVE 2.0

pyHIVE is a health-related image visualizationand engineering system using Python

My first research experience is about upgrading pyHIVE

experience

Before my sophomore summer vacation, I don't know nothing about Bioinformatics, nor getting well research trainging. At the end of that semester, One of my teacher sent me an email and said there are a sharing session about studying abroad in her lab that maybe I interested. I was free that time so I participated.

I was looking for an opportunity to fill my research experience that time. After that meeting, I talked with the professor in charge of the laboratory for a while. He told me that there is a medical image analysis software that needs to be upgraded. If I can add some more accurate algorithms and upgrade related functions, he will be glad to give me this opportunity.

So I started working with the teacher who sent me the email. I didn't learn python at that time. In fact, looking back now, it was a mess at the beginning. We ran the program on the CPU of our laptop. At that time, I knew nothing about virtualenv, which caused a lot of trouble for my later thesis writing.In fact, a lot of troubles are caused by the lack of design in the beginning. It does not mean that we have a clear software function goal, which means that we do not need to design the experimental process. But fortunately, after a series of messing up and waiting anxiously, I also developed the ability to withstand stress. Halfway through the experiment, I had new ideas. Through this, I developed the ability to multi-thread processing (and the ability to withstand double pressure)

Finally, I successfully added six new feature extraction algorithms to the original software, and achieved better accuracy than the original algorithm on the gastroscope data set. I also added a feature screening mechanism to the original software, integrating feature data obtained by two different extraction algorithms through the ttest method and filtering them by randomly removing one of the columns.

From start to finish papers I spent half a year, now I probably already familiar with this process,and can avoid a lot of detours.
